## Design Context

### Users

General fitness users building a sustainable workout habit. They use Momentum to keep a clear routine, track progress, and reduce friction around deciding what to do next.

### Brand Personality

Premium, disciplined, encouraging. The product should feel focused and self-possessed rather than playful or clinical.

### Aesthetic Direction

Follow the existing Kinetic Editorial direction from `DESIGN.md`: high contrast, strong typographic hierarchy, intentional asymmetry, and minimal chrome. The settings experience should feel like part of the same premium training environment, not a generic mobile preferences list.

### Design Principles

- Make progress feel editorial and deliberate, not administrative.
- Prefer bold hierarchy and generous negative space over dense settings lists.
- Keep controls tactile and obvious, especially for workout cadence and reminder time.
- Use inline guidance for empty, denied, and paused states instead of modal interruptions.
- Preserve consistency with the app's dark, high-contrast surface language.
